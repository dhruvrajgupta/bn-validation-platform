Next-generation sequencing (NGS) genomic proﬁling, including testing for combined positive score (CPS), may be considered to guide patient treatment options, including clinical trials. • The preferred chemoradiotherapy approach for ﬁt patients with locally advanced disease remains concurrent cisplatin and radiotherapy. • Cisplatin-based induction chemotherapy can be used, followed by radiation-based locoregional treatment (ie, sequential chemoRT). However, an improvement in overall survival with the incorporation of induction chemotherapy compared to proceeding directly to state-of- the-art concurrent chemoRT (cisplatin preferred, category 1) has not been established in randomized studies. • Cisplatin-based induction chemotherapy followed by high-dose, every-3-week cisplatin chemoradiotherapy is associated with toxicity concerns. 1,2 • After induction chemotherapy, multiple options can be used for the radiation-based portion of therapy, including radiotherapy alone, particularly for patients with CR after induction chemotherapy.
