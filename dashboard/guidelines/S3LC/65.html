A direct randomized comparison of hyperfractionated radiotherapy with or without simultaneous chemotherapy is still lacking. In a network meta-analysis [215], a significant survival advantage was found in an indirect comparison for hyperfractionated radiotherapy with dose escalation in combination with simultaneous chemotherapy. Due to the high logistical effort of a 7-week, twice-daily radiation regimen, not least for the patients, and the lack of convincing data, hyperfractionated radiotherapy with dose escalation has hardly been used in practice. Doses of over 70 Gy in this area are critical [216].
